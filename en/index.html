How does the simulator work?

This page explains how the site works, the rules used for group standings, the best 3rd-placed teams and the knockout bracket.

🚀 Go straight to the simulator
Overview

What the simulator lets you do

🏆 It’s the tool to simulate who will lift the trophy!

  • rank the teams in each group in classic mode (drag & drop);
  • either pick or automatically compute the 8 best 3rd-placed teams;
  • generate a 32-team bracket for the knockout stage;
  • switch to expert mode to predict the exact scores of every group match and fill in the knockout bracket using scores;
  • simulate each knockout round all the way to the champion.
Classic mode

Simple group standings

In classic mode, you reorder the teams in every group using drag & drop:

  • the 12 group winners qualify;
  • the 12 runners-up also qualify;
  • you then manually choose the 8 qualified third-placed teams.

The 32-team bracket is then generated, and you can click on each team to choose the winner of every match.

Expert mode

Predict every score

In expert mode, you enter the scores of every group match. The site automatically calculates for each team:

  • games played;
  • wins, draws, losses;
  • goals scored / conceded and goal difference;
  • points in the standings.

The standings in each group are updated live based on the results you enter.

Best third-placed teams

How are the 8 best 3rd-placed teams chosen?

In expert mode, once all scores are entered, the 12 teams that finished 3rd are ranked automatically according to:

  • points;
  • goal difference;
  • goals scored;

If several teams are still completely tied, the simulator shows a small window so you can separate them using fair play (you rank them from most to least fair-play).

The top 8 in this table qualify for the knockout stage.

Play-off spots

Handling play-off winners

Some World Cup spots are still unknown (labels such as “UEFA play-off winner A”, “Inter-confederation play-off winner”, etc.).

You can open the “Choose play-off winners” window and replace these placeholders with the teams you think will qualify (Italy, Ukraine, Sweden, etc.).

The name and flag are then updated everywhere:

  • in the groups (classic mode);
  • in the matches in expert mode;
  • in the knockout bracket.
Note

Limitations & important notice

This simulator is an unofficial tool, created for fun and to make predictions with friends easier.

It aims to be as accurate as possible (draws, crazy tie-breakers, etc.), but if you want to be 100% sure about the rules, you should always refer to the official competition regulations on the official website.
